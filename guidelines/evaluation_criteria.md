# Evaluation Criteria

This document defines the criteria used to evaluate AI-generated responses in this project.

## 1. Accuracy
- Is the information factually correct?
- Does the response avoid misinformation?

## 2. Clarity
- Is the response easy to understand?
- Is the language clear and well-structured?

## 3. Completeness
- Does the response fully answer the question?
- Are important details missing?

## 4. Relevance
- Does the response stay focused on the question?
- Does it avoid unnecessary or unrelated information?

---

## Scoring System

Each response is evaluated using a scale from **1 to 5**:

- **1 (Poor)** → Incorrect or unusable  
- **2 (Weak)** → Major issues  
- **3 (Acceptable)** → Basic but limited  
- **4 (Good)** → Minor issues  
- **5 (Excellent)** → Accurate, clear, and complete  

---

## Evaluation Guidelines

- Always follow the same criteria for consistency  
- Avoid subjective judgments without justification  
- Prioritize accuracy over verbosity  
- When in doubt, choose the most precise and complete response  

---

## Purpose

These criteria simulate real-world AI training workflows used in data annotation and model evaluation tasks.
